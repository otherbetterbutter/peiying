XIN

GEHA — BRANDING

This project was created for GEHA with the goal of attracting younger volunteers through vibrant and engaging collateral. The campaign, titled Xin, is rooted in the belief that courage can break down barriers and build harmony between generations.

Why Xin?
Derived from the Chinese character “心” (which means “heart”), the Xin logo features warm, flowing lines that symbolize empathy and approachability. The branding is designed to resonate emotionally while staying visually inviting for a younger audience.

Understanding Before Action
Research showed that Gen Z are keen to connect with older adults, but lack understanding or feel unsure how to approach these interactions.

PROCESS

Building bridges across generations

With XIN, I crafted a research-driven brand experience rooted in compassion and connection. Site visits and interviews with elderly individuals shaped the foundation, revealing key emotional gaps between Gen Z and older adults.

Framing empathy as the starting point, I designed a three-phase journey: Pre-Engagement met volunteers where they are—on platforms like TikTok and Instagram. Engagement brought empathy to life through mascots, games, and volunteer kits. Post-Engagement sustained connection with personalized insights and a digital community hub. Shortlisted for client presentation, XIN affirms the power of design to not just inform—but transform human connection.
