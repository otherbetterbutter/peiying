Spotify — Advertisement

Conceived as a speculative ad campaign, Spotify, Let Loose reframes music streaming as more than entertainment—it becomes a form of self-liberation in a hyper-paced urban world.

Why This Project?
In fast-moving cities, people often forget to pause. This campaign was my interpretation of Spotify’s core promise: music as a tool to relax, reset, and reawaken joy. By positioning Spotify as a pocket-sized permission to unwind, the ad encourages users to dance through the chaos—literally.

Understanding Before Action
While Spotify’s brand is often associated with playlists and productivity, I wanted to shift focus to music as release. Research showed that for many urban dwellers, the pressure to perform often overrides the instinct to simply enjoy the moment. Spotify, Let Loose responds with a visual narrative of movement, spontaneity, and personal escape—reminding us that letting go can be an act of strength.

OUTPUTS

PROCESS

From concept to camera

This project began with a question: what does it look like to unwind in a world that never stops moving? From that spark, I crafted Spotify, Let Loose as an ad campaign set in the everyday energy of Haji Lane—where stillness is rare, but music offers pause.

I led the creative direction from scripting to scene planning and visual rhythm. The process involved translating emotion into visuals—wide shots to capture movement, close-ups for intimacy, and pacing that mirrors the rising volume of the track. I focused on showing—not telling—how music can shift energy: from walking to dancing, from background noise to emotional presence.

Every frame was intentional: the flip of hair, the tap of fingers, the beat syncing with daily life. Through this campaign, I wanted to turn a simple message—press play, let loose—into a visually immersive experience.
