Design Processx

My design process has three main steps: Research, where I learn about the project; Design, where I come up with ideas; and Final Outputs, where I polish and finish the design. Each step builds on the one before to make sure the final design meets the project goals.

Problem Statement

"Singaporeans aged 20-35 are open to learning about their heritage but need engaging, accessible ways and seamless integration into their lifestyle to understand their heritage better because there are many distractions and exciting content that compete for their attention on a daily basis."

How might we

"How might we design and introduce accessible and convenient methods for Singaporeans to learn about their heritage and engage with heritage landmarks, in a way that seamlessly fits into their fast-paced lifestyles?"

Research

In the initial phase of my design process, I delved into thorough research by immersing myself in the rich heritage of Singapore, supplementing textual understanding with insights from the target audience. Understanding of Singaporeans' perspectives on heritage, lifestyles, and habits, forming a solid foundation for crafting designs tailored to capture the audience's interest.

Designxx

Focused on three key descriptors—sleek, modern, and fun—the design phase informed the visual identity of Heritage Miles. Experimentation with tones and colors culminated in the deliberate selection of an orange gradient that resonates with the brand's ethos.

Outputxx

The design process's tangible outputs included meticulously crafted table mats featuring the brand's monogram. Strategically arranged alongside essential items such as laptops, iPads, smartwatches, and NFC tags, this visual showcase communicated the app's seamless integration into contemporary lifestyles.
